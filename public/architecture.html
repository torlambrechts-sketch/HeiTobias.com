The thing that makes it one product
⟶  The Talent Data Spine — two co-equal entities

Most platforms have one spine: the person. That's the mistake. Fit, prediction and guidance are all comparisons — and a comparison needs two sides. The Role Profile is the measuring stick; the Person Profile is what gets measured. Neither is meaningful alone. This is the most overlooked entity in the entire category, and making it first-class is a genuine differentiator.

ENTITY A · the target
Role Profile
Weighted competency requirements Trait target ranges — bands, not "more is better" Calibrated cognitive demand (defensible) Context: autonomy · ambiguity · pace · stakeholder load Concrete success criteria for this role Evolution vector — where the role is heading Team-authored — co-defined, divergence surfaced Team-gap context — what's missing around it
team-defined · job-relevant · versioned = legal armor
FIT
scored, predicted,
guided — over a lifetime
ENTITY B · what's measured
Person Profile
Cognitive ability scores Personality / work-style traits Values & motivational drivers Strengths & predictable friction points Live pulse signals (ongoing, not annual) Performance outcomes over tenure Growth trajectory vs. role evolution
seeded at assessment · enriched continuously · employee-consented

The accumulated Person × Role × Outcome triples are what train sharper predictions over time — competitors who store only the person, or only the role, can never assemble this. The role's evolution vector is also the conceptual bridge that makes "hire → develop → grow" one continuous product instead of two: you develop people toward where the role is going.

How the data flows through a lifetime
STAGE 00
Define
The team co-defines the role: criteria, weights, gaps. Divergence surfaced & reconciled.
STAGE 01
Assess
Finalists measured against role and team-gap. Profile created.
STAGE 02
Hire & onboard
Decision report + auto-generated tailored 90-day plan for the manager.
STAGE 03
Manage
Living profile drives weekly guidance, 1:1 prep, team-fit insight.
STAGE 04
Re-fit & grow
Periodic re-evaluation: person-drift vs. role-drift → the fit trajectory.
↻ FEEDBACK LOOP: performance outcomes from Stage 03–04 retrain the predictive models in Layer 03 — every hire makes the next prediction sharper.
Fit is a trajectory, not a snapshot
Re-evaluation over time
Both sides drift. Re-measure the person against the (evolved) role on a cadence. The gap between person-drift and role-drift is the most actionable signal in the system.
Growth gaprole moved ahead → develop
Flight riskperson outgrew role → promote
Stable fitwell-matched → leave alone
Emerging misfitdiverging → intervene early
consent-owned · developmental framing, never surveillance
Team-based role definition
The team that will work with the role co-defines it — not one manager's mental model of "someone like me."
Multiple evaluators rate criteria importance independently Reconciliation flow surfaces disagreement (the useful signal) Candidate scored vs. role + existing team composition gap Collective definition = documented, bias-reduced, AI-Act defensible
⚠ team composition from members' own profiles — NOT peers rating peers
The recruiter is the wedge — land & expand

The hardest problem in the lifecycle model is seeding the spine and getting employers onto the post-hire layer. Recruiters solve it. They already do assessment & role-definition work during placements — that work is your seed data. At placement it travels to the employer, who arrives pre-populated instead of blank. The agency becomes the customer-acquisition engine.

AGENCY · acquisition layer

Model 1 — Hand-over (acquires new employers)
Agency & employer have separate instances. At placement, the profile transfers (consent-gated). This is how agencies bring you employers who aren't customers yet.
Model 2 — Shared workspace (stickier)
Employer is already on-platform; agency is invited into a requisition as a scoped collaborator. Data is simply already there at close. No transfer.
⚠ DESIGN-IN FROM SCHEMA: candidate-owned, consent-gated portability (3-party GDPR: agency · candidate · employer) · quality floors so weak agencies can't seed garbage · clear channel posture to avoid disintermediation fear.
Why this unifies the whole business
⟶  One funnel, not two products

Recruiting and lifecycle stopped being competing ideas. The recruiting product is the acquisition layer; the lifecycle product is the retention & expansion layer — land-and-expand built into the structure. This is also where your three strengths finally converge: recruiting sells the agency side, leadership/management delivers the employer value, scale-up building runs the channel-led growth motion.
